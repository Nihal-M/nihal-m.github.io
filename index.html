Nihal Murali

I’m a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate in the Intelligent Systems Program at the University of Pittsburgh, jointly advised by Prof. Kayhan Batmanghelich and Shyam Visweswaran. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, medicine, and human–AI collaboration—developing algorithms that make AI systems safer, more interpretable, and clinically reliable. Broadly, I study how AI models behave under uncertainty and how they interact with human expertise in high-stakes decision making. My work spans from understanding model failures such as shortcut and spurious learning, to designing hybrid human–AI systems that integrate algorithmic predictions with human judgment for expert-level performance in healthcare applications.

I was a visiting researcher in the Machine Learning Research Group (MLRG) at the University of Guelph, advised by Prof. Graham W. Taylor, where I worked on computer-vision models for biological data in collaboration with Prof. Joel D. Levine. Our work (PLOS ONE 2018; WACV 2019) showed that deep convolutional networks can recognize and re-identify individual Drosophila melanogaster across days, bridging biological vision and AI. Previously, I interned at the Robotics Research Center (RRC), International Institute of Information Technology, Hyderabad, where I worked with Prof. Madhav Krishna and Dr. Krishna Murthy on robotics and perception.

I received my M.E. degree in Software Systems and B.E. (Hons.) degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from BITS Pilani, where I was advised by Prof. Surekha Bhanot.
